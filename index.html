Frequently Asked Questions

What are the key components needed for the seismometer car's autopilot system?

To get the autopilot working, you'll need a GPS, accelerometers, gyroscopes, a microcontroller, and some smart software for navigation and avoiding obstacles

How will the car's flying mechanism be integrated with the ground navigation system?

The flying part will seamlessly switch to ground navigation using a hybrid control system that can choose the best mode based on what the sensors detect and what you command

How will the seismometer data be collected and stored?

The car will use sensitive vibration sensors to collect seismometer data, which can either be stored on the car itself or sent wirelessly to a remote server for you to analyze later

What safety features will be implemented to ensure the car can operate both on the ground and in the air?

or safety, the car will have collision detection sensors, emergency landing protocols, automatic obstacle avoidance, and backup power systems to keep it running smoothly

How does the seismometer car make use of solar power?

The car has solar panels that soak up sunlight and turn it into electricity, helping charge the batteries and keep the car running longer without needing to plug it in

What are the benefits of the car being solar-powered?

Using solar power means the car can operate for extended periods, it's environmentally friendly, and it reduces the need for frequent recharging, making it more efficient for fieldwork
